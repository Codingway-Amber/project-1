Behold! The Carina Nebula!

A wonder of the universe.

More Info

The image here was taken when NASA's James Webb Space Telescope was brand new. This is the most clear picture we've ever had of the Carina Nebula. Stars previously hidden in older images have been "brought to light," thanks to this technology!

"Why is this cloud of space dust that's quadrillions of miles away even important!?" I hear you ask.

If you must know, allow me to explain.

Here's a few reasons why:

  1. It's 7 light-years high!!
  2. The nebula is located around 7,600 light-years from earth!
  3. The region constantly births new stars, thanks to the massive amount of gas and dust which move very slowly.

Some more reasons:

  • While this visual is famous, it's actually a zoomed in portion of the nebula. It's called NGC 3324!
  • It's 4 million times more luminous than the sun!
  • It's simply beautiful, what other reasons do you still need!?
